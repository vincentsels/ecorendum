The global context

In order to prevent the of climate change, almost every country in the world signed the Paris Climate Agreement, agreeing to keep global warming well below 2°C, and aiming for less than 1.5°C.

The European targets

Every country must do its bit. Belgium follows the Companies in heavy industry and energy production buy allowances on the ETS market. For the other sectors, Belgian governments must reduce emissions by 47% from 2005 levels by 2030.

gavel

The Climate Case

Belgium and especially Flanders are not meeting the targets imposed by Europe. This threatens the human rights of future generations. That is why tens of thousands of concerned citizens filed a Climate Case against the Belgian authorities. The court proved them right, imposing on Belgium a reduction of even 55% compared to 1990 by 2030.

The impact

In response to the Climate Case, Flemish Climate and Energy Minister Zuhal Demir had the Flemish Energy and Climate Agency (VEKA) quantify the impact of the imposed targets. This is clearly very big. High time we determined democratically how we are going to get it done.

The framework conditions

The transition will not be easy, and we will have to make societal choices. How much are we willing to pay? How much risk can we take? Do measures cause other, perhaps even bigger environmental problems? Is the transition proceeding equitably? And what is the price of doing nothing?

The preferendum
